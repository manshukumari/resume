Causes of Plastic Pollution

Single-Use Plastics

Single-use plastics, like bottles and bags, contribute significantly to pollution due to their short lifespan and low recycling rates.

Microplastics

Microplastics, tiny plastic particles, enter the environment from degraded plastics and synthetic fibers, impacting marine life and entering the food chain.

Industrial Plastic Waste

Industries contribute significantly to plastic waste through packaging, production residues, and synthetic materials.

Ocean Dumping

Millions of tons of plastic end up in oceans each year, harming marine ecosystems and wildlife.

Landfills Overflowing with Plastics

Non-biodegradable plastics in landfills take hundreds of years to decompose, polluting soil and groundwater.
